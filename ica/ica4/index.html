Reflection

Identity & Culture

How do your color and typography choices reflect your personal or cultural identity?

I tried to base the rest of my color pallette off of a pink since that's my favorite color. I feel like the vibrant colors represent my outgoing personality. For my font choices, I wanted to choose one serif font and one sans-serif font. I think the sans-serif font that I chose shows is very uniform, orderly, and neat, which is how I like to represent the work that I do. The serif font I chose I can see as a more "fun" font while still being very neat.

What visual elements feel most authentic to you and why?

The visual elements that feel most authentic to me are my color and font choices for the reasons that I explained earlier. Again, I feel like I try to present myself as neat and I'm an outgoing person. And I feel that the font and color choices reflect that.

Accessibility Considerations

How did accessibility requirements influence your design decisions?

I used the WebAIM WCAG contrast checker to make sure that the color pallette I chose had and acceptable contrast. I also made sure I didn't use a font that was too difficult to read, too bold, etc.

What challenges did you face balancing personal expression with accessibility?

There were certain colors that I wanted to use but I would use the WebAIM WCAG contrast checker against them and see that they were not great colors to place against white or black. There were some colors that I had to leave out of my selection so that my system could be more accessible.

Design Process

What surprised you about creating a cohesive design system?

What surprised me about creating a cohesive design system was that there is so much thought that goes into the design and a lot of thought into what you would use certain elements for. When I was choosing my fonts, I was thinking more about what would look good as heading vs body text. I was also surprised how much a design changes once you try to take accessibility into account.

How will you apply this system to your portfolio project?

I will apply this system to my portfolio project by using my new selected fonts for the text, creating different divs with background colors from the pallette I chose, and ensuring that the spacing between each of my elements matches my design system so that elements can be visually grouped together a bit more cleanly.

Future Applications

How might you expand or refine this system for different contexts?

I might refine this system by adding different font weights depending on the use of the text. I could also look into which colors I would use together (which to use as a background vs foreground color).

What aspects of your system feel most/least flexible?

I feel like the most flexible or most applicable part of my system is the spacing. It can be applied to many different contexts on a website and it's a pretty reasonable system for most cases. The least flexible part of my system I think would be the colors. If I were trying to have a more serious tone instead of a fun one, I'd have to leave some colors out.
